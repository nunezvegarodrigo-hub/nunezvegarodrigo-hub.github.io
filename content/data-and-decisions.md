# Data and Decision-Making

"I wish we knew how we compare to other legal departments." Most teams say it. Few can answer it. Not because the data is missing, but because nobody turned it into a decision.

Data does not replace judgment. It sharpens it. The point of a number is to change what you do next.

## Measure what matters

Most legal dashboards are ignored. They show volume. Contracts processed. Tickets closed. Volume is activity, not value. Two hundred contracts reviewed tells you nothing if you cannot say how fast, how risky, or how much it cost.

Measure cycle time, rework, cost, and risk. Those are the numbers leadership already cares about. Build the dashboard around the decision, not the data you happen to have.

## Get leadership to read it

A dashboard nobody acts on is decoration. The gap between a middle-level report and an executive one is not more charts. It is fewer, clearer, tied to a call someone has to make.

- **Presenting data to executives is a skill, not a formatting exercise.** Lead with the decision, then the number, then the detail.
- **Bridge the decision gap.** In fast-moving teams, decisions drift between levels until no one owns them. Data closes that gap by making the tradeoff visible.
- **Decision-making is a team sport.** Operational, tactical, strategic. Each level needs a different view of the same truth.
