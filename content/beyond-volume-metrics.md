---
title: "Moving Beyond Volume Metrics: Quantity Is Not Value"
tags:
  - data-and-decisions
---

"We processed 200 contracts this month." Sounds impressive. But what does it actually tell you? Nothing about quality. Nothing about risk. Nothing about efficiency. Nothing about value.

Volume metrics are vanity metrics. They make you feel busy. They do not tell you if you are improving.

Start tracking what actually matters.

- **Time per contract.** Not total volume. How long does each one take? That reveals bottlenecks.
- **Escalation rate.** What percentage of requests reach the GC's desk? High escalation means an unclear process or insufficient authority.
- **Deviation frequency.** How often does your team leave the standard playbook? Sometimes it is necessary. Often it is inefficiency.
- **Rework rate.** How many contracts go through three, four, five rounds of redlines? Each round is wasted time.

Quantity tells you how much you did. Quality tells you how well you did it. Value tells you whether it mattered.

Stop celebrating volume. Start measuring what actually drives improvement.
