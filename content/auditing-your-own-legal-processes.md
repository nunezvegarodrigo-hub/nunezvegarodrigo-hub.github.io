No one likes audits. They feel like inspections, like judgment, like someone looking for mistakes.

But here's the thing: the best audit is the one you do yourself.

If you wait for an external auditor, you've already lost the chance to fix things quietly. If you wait for a regulator, you've already lost control of the narrative. If you wait for a problem to surface, you've already lost the opportunity to prevent it.

Auditing your own processes is not defensive. It's strategic.

What to review:

- Are your playbooks current, or are they collecting dust?
- Are your contracting deviations documented and explained?
- Are your data security practices keeping pace with new tools?
- Are your escalation patterns revealing weaknesses in authority or training?

The goal is not perfection. The goal is awareness. You can't fix what you don't know is broken.

*When was the last time your team audited its own processes, not because someone asked, but because it's good practice?*
