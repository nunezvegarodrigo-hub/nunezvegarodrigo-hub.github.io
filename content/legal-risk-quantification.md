"High risk. Medium risk. Low risk."

We've all used these labels. They're simple. They're easy. They're also nearly useless.

Because "high risk" means different things to different people. To a lawyer, it might mean litigation exposure. To a finance executive, it means dollars. To a CEO, it means reputational damage.

If you want the business to care about risk, you have to speak their language. And their language is money.

Legal risk quantification means assigning financial impact to legal risk.

- A contract deviation that exposes you to a $2 million claim.
- A compliance gap that could trigger a $500,000 fine.
- A missed renewal that costs $100,000 in automatic fees.

Now the business understands. Now you're not just "flagging risk." You're showing cost. You're showing exposure. You're showing trade-offs.

You don't need perfect data to start. You need a methodology. Start with estimates. Refine over time.
