Remember when vendor due diligence meant checking financials and asking for SOC 2 reports?

Those days are gone.

AI vendors introduce new risks that traditional due diligence doesn't cover.

**Hallucinations.** The vendor's AI might invent facts, cite non-existent cases, or produce confident nonsense. Your team needs to know how the vendor detects and mitigates hallucinations.

**Data leakage.** What happens to the data you upload? Is it used to train their models? Is it stored securely? Does it leave your control? These questions need explicit answers.

**Bias.** Does the vendor's AI produce different results based on irrelevant factors? Has it been tested for fairness? Does the vendor have a bias mitigation strategy?

**Regulatory exposure.** Does the vendor comply with relevant regulations? GDPR? PIPEDA? Industry-specific rules? Who is accountable when something goes wrong?

Third-party risk in the AI era requires new questions.

Your vendor contract should address: data usage rights, model transparency, liability for hallucinations, security protocols, and exit strategy.

The vendor you choose today will shape your risk profile for years.
