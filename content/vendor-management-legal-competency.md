Vendor contracts are everywhere. SaaS tools. Professional services. Office supplies. IT infrastructure. Marketing agencies. Most legal teams review them one by one, deal by deal. No standardization. No leverage. No institutional memory. That is not vendor management. That is whack-a-mole.

True vendor management as a legal competency requires four things.

- **Standardized fallback positions.** Not every vendor gets a unique negotiation. Define your standard positions on liability, data security, termination, and IP. Use them consistently.
- **Tiered review processes.** Low-risk, low-spend vendors move fast. High-risk, strategic vendors get deeper review. Match effort to exposure.
- **A living vendor playbook.** What concessions did you make last time? What terms did you reject? What data security questions did you ask? Preserve the knowledge.
- **Ongoing monitoring, not just intake.** A vendor that passed review two years ago may look different today. Financial health changes. Security practices change. Ownership changes. Monitor continuously.

Vendor management is not administrative. It is strategic infrastructure for risk and leverage.
