title: Benchmarking Legal Performance Without Losing Your Mind

"I wish we knew how we compare to other legal departments." I hear this constantly. And then teams spend months chasing benchmarks that do not fit their context.

Benchmarking is useful. But done wrong, it is dangerous. The problem: every legal department is different. Different industry. Different scale. Different risk tolerance. Different leadership expectations. Comparing your contract turnaround time to a tech company's when you work in manufacturing? Misleading. Comparing your external spend to a bank's when you are in retail? Pointless.

So what should you compare?

- **First, benchmark against yourself.** Last quarter versus this quarter. Last year versus this year. Are you improving? That is the most important comparison.
- **Second, benchmark against the business need.** Is your turnaround time fast enough for sales? Is your cost low enough for finance? Internal satisfaction matters more than external rankings.
- **Third, use external benchmarks carefully.** Find peers in your industry. Adjust for context. Take everything with skepticism.

The goal of benchmarking is not to win. It is to get less bad, consistently, over time.
