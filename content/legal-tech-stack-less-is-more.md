title: "The Legal Tech Stack: Less Is More"
tags:
  - legal-ops
---

I've seen legal departments with fifteen different tools. Contract review. Document management. E-signature. Intake. Workflow automation. AI analysis. Repository. Reporting. Clause library. Task tracking. Fifteen tools. Five logins. Three data silos. No integration. No adoption. No results.

A complex stack is not sophisticated. It is broken. The best legal tech stack is not the one with the most tools. It is the one that actually gets used.

- **One source of truth.** One place where contracts live. One place where requests are tracked. One place where data is reported. If you have multiple, you have chaos.
- **Integration over features.** Five tools that talk to each other beat one perfect tool that sits alone. Buy for the ecosystem, not the demo.
- **Adoption over capability.** The most powerful tool does nothing if no one uses it. Prioritize tools your team will actually open.
- **Elimination before addition.** Before adding a new tool, ask what you can retire. Legal tech debt compounds. Clean as you go.

A lean stack that works beats a comprehensive stack that does not.
